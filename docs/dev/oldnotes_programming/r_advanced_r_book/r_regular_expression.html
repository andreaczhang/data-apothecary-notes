Regular expressions

combination of literals (i.e. words) and metacharacters (i.e. grammar)

matching literals (exact words) are not enough, we need a way to express

  • whitespace
  • sets of literals
  • Beginning and end of a line
  • alternatives (‘war’ or ‘peace’)
  • …

use meta characters!

^: start of a line

^i think matches

i think we are ..

i think this …

$: end of a line

morning$ matches

well they had something this morning

good morning

[] character classes

[Bb][Uu][Ss][Hh] matches

Bush

bush

^[Ii] am matches I am, i am

^[0-9][a-zA-Z] matches 7th …, 2nd…, 3am…, …. (line starts with one number and one letter)

^ inside []: Not in the class

[^?.]$ matches characters/sentences that do NOT end with ? or .

i like basketballs

6 and 9

anyway!

.: any character

9.11 matches

9-11

9.11

169.114

9:11:46AM

… 8199119725 …

\.: period

(. in this case is not a meta character, but a literal character)

?: optional (outside [])

[Gg]eorge( [Ww]\.)? [Bb]ush

george bush

George W. Bush

two george bushes

|: or

flood|fire matches firewire …, global flood makes sense,…, floods, horricanes

^[Gg]ood|[Bb]ad means good/Good needs to be at the beginning, or Bad/bad anywhere

^([Gg]ood|[Bb]ad) means good/Good and Bad/bad need to be at the beginning

*: any number, including none

(.*) matches

chat? (24, m, germany)

()

+: at least one of the item

[0-9]+ (.*)[0-9]+

means a number can be repeated many times

{}: inerval

[Bb]ush( +[^ ]+ +){1, 5} debate

at least one space, followed by NOT space, followed by a space === a word, repeated between 1 and 5 times

Bush has historically won all major debates he’s done
