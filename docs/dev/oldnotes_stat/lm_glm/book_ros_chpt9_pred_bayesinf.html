prediction (bayesian)

point prediction: \(\hat{a} + \hat{b}x^{new}\)

  • one point, no uncertainty
  • e.g. point prediction is the best estimate of the average blood pressure in the population, conditional on dose \(x^{new}\)

Linear predictor with uncertainty: \(a + bx^{new}\)

  • Contains inferential uncertainty in a, b
  • distribution of uncertainty of expected or average \(y\) for a new observation
  • n -> inf, uncertainty -> 0 as a and b are more and more precise

predictive distribution for a new observation: \(a + bx^{new} + error\)

  • uncertainty about new obervation

  • blood pressure of a single person drawn at random from this population

  • n->inf, uncertainty -> residual standard deviation sigma

priors

weekly informative prior (default)

  • linear model:
    • bk ~N(0, 2.5*sd(y)/sd(xk))
    • a ~ N(mu_y, 2.5*sd(y))
    • sigma ~ exp(1/sd(y))

check prior: prior_summary()
